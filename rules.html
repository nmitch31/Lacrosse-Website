The Rules of Lacrosse
The sport of men's lacrosse is played on two feet. Each player has their own stick, and there is one ball. It is typically played on a grass or turf field, although there is a version of the sport called "box lacrosse" that is played indoors.
There are 10 players on each team, consisting of 3 attackman, 3 midfielders (aka middies), 3 defenseman, and one goalie. There is a goal on either end, a midline, two box lines, and two face off wing lines.
Only 6 players are allowed on either side of the midline, so while any position is allowed to play on the offensive half, 4 players at a time must stay on the defensive half. There are 4 long sticks allowed on the field at a time, and 1 goalie stick. This means one midfielder can be designated as "long stick midfielder" or "LSM". Teams will then switch the LSM with a short stick midfielder when they transition from defense to offense, as short sticks are more advantageous on offense; avoiding defenseman's checks is easier.
The goal of the game is to score goals by shooting the ball into the net. Teams are awarded one point when they score a goal. The team with the most points after 4 quarters of play is the winner.
Defense is played with body and stick. Excessive use will warrant penalty calls. The rules breakdown by level of play is listed below. You cannot touch the ball with your hand. There is a goalie crease around the goal, which offensive players are not allowed to step inside. They also cannot touch the goalie while he is inside.
